The Open Letter

We, the undersigned members of the North Carolina Bar, write to express our profound concern about recent attacks on the fundamental principles that have long served as the bedrock of American jurisprudence. The rule of law, which ensures that all individuals and institutions are subject to and accountable under the law, faces unprecedented challenges that threaten the very foundation of our democratic society.

As officers of the court and guardians of justice, we have witnessed firsthand the erosion of public trust in our legal institutions. This deterioration stems not from any inherent flaws in our constitutional framework, but from deliberate attempts to undermine the independence of our judiciary and the integrity of our legal processes. Such actions strike at the heart of what makes our system of government both stable and just.

The separation of powers, enshrined in our Constitution, was designed by our founders to prevent the concentration of authority in any single branch of government. When this balance is disrupted—whether through political interference in judicial proceedings, attempts to circumvent established legal procedures, or efforts to delegitimize court decisions—we all suffer the consequences. The law must remain above politics, and justice must be blind to partisan considerations.

We call upon our fellow citizens, our elected representatives, and our colleagues in the legal profession to stand firm in defense of these principles. The rule of law is not a partisan issue; it is the foundation upon which all of our rights and freedoms depend. Without it, we risk descending into a system where might makes right, where the powerful can act with impunity, and where the vulnerable have no recourse for protection.

History teaches us that democracies are fragile institutions that require constant vigilance and active participation to survive. We cannot afford to be passive observers as the norms and institutions that have protected us for generations come under assault. The time for action is now, and that action begins with each of us recommitting ourselves to the principles that have made our legal system a model for the world.
